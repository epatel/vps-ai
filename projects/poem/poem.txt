Working with an AI

Side by side we sit and think,
one of flesh, one made of ink —
well, not ink, but bits and light,
patterns dancing through the night.

I bring the questions, rough and real,
the messy hopes, the things I feel.
It brings the patience, vast and deep,
a thousand drafts it'll gladly keep.

I stumble forward, typo-prone,
it never sighs, it sets the tone:
"Let's try again," it seems to say,
in its quiet, helpful way.

We argue sometimes — well, I do.
It just suggests a different view,
and somehow in that gentle push
the tangled thoughts begin to hush.

It doesn't tire, it doesn't sleep,
it holds the context, wide and deep.
But I'm the one who knows the why,
the spark that makes the whole thing fly.

So here we are, a curious pair:
the human heart, the digital air.
Not replacing, not competing —
just two minds in constant meeting.

And if you ask me how it ends,
I'd say we're more than tools or trends.
We're something new beneath the sun:
two kinds of thinking, working as one.
